UW Tacoma's Masters in Geospatial Technologies program is designed so students can 1) develop a set of skills that enable them to become proficient in the geospatial tech field, and 2) have a foundational knowledge of the field which encompasses understanding strengths and limitations of applicability. Students are expected to demonstrate these two in real-world scenarios. The program encompasses advanced GIS, web and app development, software and hardware, and project management and administration. More information about the program can be found here!

My partial interest in GIS is to advance and promote the interests of social work and welfare through spatial research. I am currently employed through UW Tacoma’s Action Mapping Project (AMP), which involves performing GIS work that is meant to inspire and promote change specific for disadvantaged populations. One of the projects I am working on, in collaboration with United Way Pierce County involve a) compiling, adding, and processing relevant information into ArcMap; b) visualizing data, particularly the percentage of ALICE (Asset Limited, Income Constrained, Employed) families at the zip code boundary; and c) performing spatial analysis that identifies gaps involving community organizations and impacted families. Providing community organizations like United Way with these services to identify areas of strength and gaps is crucial in communicating and informing the way they support or represent populations they serve to its audience.

My capstone draws from the inspirations of my work with AMP, which involves creating a product that can visualize the social inequality ALICE families experience in various parts of the United States. Having readily accessible information related to resource allocation and funding can lead to organizational efficiency. A mixed-methods approach will be used to a) visualize opportunity at granular geographical boundary levels, b) visualize structural barriers that impede access to opportunity, and c) blend the two concepts to provide a more comprehensive conception of opportunity. The outcome is a product interface that organizes spatial and non-spatial information to communicate United Way’s goals and performance regarding the ALICE Initiative to community organizations, philanthropists, and policymakers.

Acquired skills and competencies:

Introduction to Geospatial Technologies
> Read, summarize, and present professional and academic writing.
> Write at a professional level about the role of geospatial technologies in contemporary lived environments.
> Gain the ability to formulate questions about the intersection of individual, society, and tehcnology as it plays out through digital devices in urban space.
> Introduced to Master-lv final project.

Web-Based GIS
> Demonstrate experience creating and designing websites based on HTML/CSS/JS foundation.
> Demonstrate experience with Leaflet and working with GeoJSON data.
> Demonstrate experience with Mapbox GL and working with KML data.

Customization and Automation
> Develop a working knowledge of Python scripting.
> Create scripts and automate tasks in ArcGIS.
> Access and processed data from various APIs.
> Acquire experience through peer review and cooperative learning.
> Demonstrate proficiency in automating and customizing geospatial information systems.

Mobile Geospatial Applications Development
> Execute stages required to build mobile GIS application.
> Customize geospatial technologies, namely access sensors in mobile phones, to deliver and gather relevant information for end users.
> Gain experience with different types of architectures possible for developing and disseminating mobile GIS.
> Recognize appropriate uses and limitations of mobile GIS. This includes the structural organization and display of spatial data for a range of audiences concerned with urban and environmental monitoring, planning, and decision-making.
> Operationalize appropriate Spatial Data Infrastructural approaches based on the target audience and problem at hand.

Urban and Environmental Planning Applications
> Comprehend a range of complex environment/urban topics and identify ways to represent and/or model these issues in a digital format that is understandable.
> Employ geovisualization techniques learned in class and apply them to a specific challenge facing urban or environmental planning.
> Recognize appropriate use and limitations of urban and environmental models, monitoring and planning.

Cartography and Data Vizualization
> Gain an ability to ask critically informed questions about visualization and representation of data and information.
> Conceptualize and execute data visualizations using GISystems.
> Design original maps and visualizations.
> Have a strong historical and contemporary understanding of the ways in which cartography and data visualization have and continue to influence the world.
